Eunice

Eunice is a fully standardized, modular, AI-driven research platform designed for systematic literature reviews, research planning, and academic project management.

All services follow identical structure patterns with successful MCP (Model Context Protocol) integration for write operations and direct database access for read operations, ensuring optimal performance and data consistency.

The platform implements a hierarchical research structure: Projects → Research Topics → Research Plans → Tasks, enabling structured and scalable research workflows.

Each functional component runs in its own standardized Docker container with multi-stage builds, comprehensive health monitoring, and security hardening.

Brain

This project integrates biology, artificial intelligence, and advanced engineering to develop a self-contained research ecosystem capable of modelling, interfacing, and automating complex biological and robotic systems.

At its biological core is ZMEM, a custom, low-cost cell culture medium derived from readily available compounds to replicate the nutrient composition of commercial DMEM. ZMEM supports experiments in cellular reprogramming, particularly transforming keratinocytes and fibroblasts into neurons using small-molecule protocols, with bovine colostrum serving as a serum substitute.

On the computational side, the project develops a Research Assistant (RA)—a locally hosted AI framework for scientific reasoning, data retrieval, and automated literature analysis. The RA system uses custom tokenisation, reasoning datasets, and model architectures optimised for domain-specific inference and interpretability.

Parallel engineering efforts support both biological and AI experimentation through custom hardware platforms. These include precision mechanical components (rotors, actuators, and servo drives) for the LD-6 “Ludo” biped robot, and autonomous aerial systems such as an ISR micro-drone deployable from moving vehicles and integrated with open-source flight software (Arduflight, MAVLink) and situational-awareness frameworks (Anduril Lattice OS).

Collectively, the project aims to unify wet-lab experimentation, machine intelligence, and robotic embodiment into a coherent, secure, and fully local research environment. It emphasises reproducibility, cost-efficiency, and independence from proprietary systems, providing a foundation for future work in synthetic neurobiology, embodied cognition, and autonomous research platforms.
