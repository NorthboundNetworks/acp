Custom Actuator Specifications and Manufacturing Guide

1. General Overview

The actuator is designed to match Unitree actuator dimensions for drop-in compatibility, with an outer diameter (OD) of 95 mm, wall thickness of 2 mm, and total depth ~42 mm. This avoids robot joint redesign.

  • Actuator type: Brushless outrunner with integrated cycloidal gearbox.
  • Target torque: ~25–30 Nm peak.
  • Voltage: 24V nominal.
  • Form factor: Based on Unitree motor CAD (95 mm OD).

2. Motor Design

Rotor

  • Material: Soft magnetic steel ring (laminated if possible).
  • Magnets: NdFeB (N52 recommended), outer surface mounted, arc-shaped for minimal cogging.
  • Rotor OD: 85 mm (within 95 mm case).
  • Rotor fabrication: Bar stock bent into ring, welded, and machined to tolerance.

Stator

  • Construction: Segmented teeth, laminated stacks bonded to back iron ring.
  • Material: Laser-cut electrical steel laminations (0.55 mm thick, locally available).
  • Stack length: ~27–30 mm to balance torque and copper fill.
  • Winding: Copper wire, enamel insulated, wound on individual teeth before assembly.

Bearings & Shaft

  • Bearings: Dual angular contact bearings (to withstand radial + axial loads).
  • Shaft: Hardened steel, precision ground.

3. Gearbox (Cycloidal Drive)

  • Type: Double-disc anti-backlash cycloidal gearbox.
  • Input shaft: Direct from motor rotor.
  • Reduction ratio: ~1:15 to 1:20.
  • Materials:
    • Discs: High-strength steel (laser-cut + machined).
    • Rollers/pins: Hardened steel dowels, ground finish.
    • Output plate: Aluminium 7075 for weight savings.
  • Inner clearance: ≥30 mm ID reserved for gearset.

4. Housing & Case

  • Outer case OD: 95 mm, 2 mm wall thickness.
  • Material: Aluminium 6061-T6 (lightweight, strong, machinable).
  • Fabrication: Bar rolled/welded into a cylinder, machined to final tolerance.
  • Cooling: Passive conduction via aluminium housing, optional finning.

5. Electronics

  • ESC (Electronic Speed Controller): FOC (field-oriented control) supported (e.g. ODrive, SimpleFOC, custom board).
  • Sensors:
    • Hall sensors for commutation (optional).
    • High-resolution encoder (magnetic/optical) for position feedback, mounted on motor shaft.
  • Voltage range: 20–28 V (24 V nominal).

6. Manufacturing Notes

Lamination Cutting & Insulation

  • Use fiber laser cutting for 0.55 mm electrical steel sheets.
  • Ensure smooth edges to minimize eddy current losses.
  • Apply insulation varnish between layers to prevent shorted turns.

Assembly

  1. Machine/weld rotor and housing rings to correct OD/ID.
  2. Stack laminated stator teeth into back iron ring, bond with epoxy.
  3. Wind copper coils on segmented teeth before insertion.
  4. Insert stator into housing, mount bearings and shaft.
  5. Assemble rotor with magnets, ensuring balanced placement.
  6. Install cycloidal gearset and output plate.
  7. Close housing with seals and fasteners.

7. Expected Performance

  • Peak torque: ~25–30 Nm (depending on winding design and reduction ratio).
  • Continuous torque: ~10–15 Nm (thermally limited).
  • Weight estimate: 400–500 g per actuator (lighter than Unitree’s 680 g).
  • Speed: ~30–40 rad/s before gearing.

8. Cost Considerations (AUD)

  • Electrical steel laminations: ~$40–60 per stator.
  • Magnets: ~$80–100 per rotor set.
  • Bearings & shaft: ~$30–50.
  • Aluminium housing material: ~$40.
  • Cycloidal drive parts: ~$70–100 (machined).
  • Electronics/encoder: ~$80–120.

Estimated unit cost (prototype, self-built): ~$300–400 AUD per actuator.

9. CAD Integration Notes

  • Maintain OD = 95 mm to ensure Unitree joint compatibility.
  • Reserve 30 mm ID clearance for cycloidal gearbox.
  • Shaft mounting: match Unitree spline/bolt pattern if reusing existing mechanical joints.
